How we select petitions

We select recommended petitions based on three key criteria, each with sub-questions:

  • Impact: What scale of positive change can this petition create?
    • How much benefit is gained by a change from the current state to the suggested state?
    • Do Key Stakeholders have influence over other important issues?
    • How important is the general issue discussed by the petition?
  • Tractability: How possible is it to achieve the desired outcome?
    • How likely is the petition to reach 'critical numbers' both with and without sharing? (either for media attention, formal action, or org legitimacy). Are these number practically possible to achieve?
    • Do Key Stakeholders have the power to fix the issue?
    • Is there surrounding work being done, so that getting the attention of Key Stakeholders turns into them taking action?
    • How easy is the petition to read, understand and act upon?
  • Neglectedness: How under-addressed or overlooked is this issue?
    • How likely is the petition to reach 'critical numbers' both with and without sharing? (either for media attention, formal action, or org legitimacy). Are these numbers challenging to achieve?
    • How not-well-understood is the general issue discussed by the petition?
